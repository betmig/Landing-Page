Explore Luleå

Luleå is a coastal city in Swedish Lapland. It’s known for the Gammelstad Church Town, a cluster of well-preserved wooden houses and the stone Nederluleå Church from the 1400s. In the city center, Norrbottens Museum showcases local history, art and culture. Offshore, in the Bay of Bothnia, are more than 1,000 islands with beaches, cottages and seals. Junkön Island has a museum and an 18th-century windmill.

Kulturens hus

Kulturens hus, in the middle of Luleå center, is a popular meeting place filled with life and desire. It is Luleå's cultural hub, creative engine and an obvious place for networking and meetings of both a personal and professional nature. Here you will find concert halls, fine conference facilities, a city library, an art gallery, a tourist office, a restaurant and a café. On the top floor of the house is also the tenant Norrbottensmusiken.

Kulturens hus is one of Norrbotten's most visited places. It has as many visitors as, for example, Kolmården, ie 500,000 people per year. With their location by Luleå's northern harbor, you can enjoy a fantastic view and beautiful summer nights with midnight sun.

Kulturens hus is 14,000 square meters large and designed by Tirsén & Aili Arkitekter with Hans Tirsén as the responsible architect. The first sod was taken in April 2005 and the house was opened on January 3, 2007 to the great joy of the people of Luleå. Luleå municipality is the principal for their nice house and the culture and leisure administration is responsible for the house's contents.

Recreations and sports

Luleå archipelago consists of several islands and has a lot to explore with its sandy beaches, historical buildings and animal life. In summer time the islands are also served by the municipal archipelago boats. In winter, when the sea freezes you can take a walk on the popular ice road from the north to the south harbor.

Located a few minutes outside of Luleå city lies Gammelstad (Luleå old town), which consists of church town with over 400 wooden houses around a medieval church, and is on the UNESCO World Heritage Site. Adjacent is the open-air museum Hägnan, with café and charming little cottages that allow you to travel back in time. Due to land elevation in the year of 1649, Gammelstad had to be moved to the present location Luleå city.

Services

Pharmacies: A doctor’s prescription is generally needed to obtain drugs at Swedish pharmacies (Apotek), except for common over-the-counter medicines, like aspirin. At pharmacies you can obtain advice on treatment of minor illnesses such as headache, cold and cough, allergic reactions, fever and stomach problems. The closest pharmacies Apoteket Hjärtat Björkskatan, Höstvägen 7, are open Monday through Friday, 8:30 a.m. to 5 p.m. Mjölkudden, Mjölkuddsvägen 73, are open Monday through Friday, 9 a.m. to 6 p.m. In Luleå Centrum there are several pharmacies and also at Storheden. You can buy certain non-prescription drugs at grocery stores.
